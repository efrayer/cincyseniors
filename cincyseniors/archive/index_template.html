This week's safety spotlight

One scam, one tip, one idea — chosen by a human.

Rotated every Monday by our community team. Here's why we picked each one.

Scam to watch

"The grandparent in trouble" voice clone

A caller who sounds exactly like your grandchild asks for money urgently. They're often using AI voice cloning from social media clips.

Why we picked this: two Cincinnati residents reported it to us this week.

Privacy tip

Don't type real names into AI chats

If you're asking the helper for advice about a family member, use "my daughter" or "my neighbor" instead of their real name. Your conversation stays useful, and their privacy stays protected.

Why we picked this: a common question at our in-person classes.

Good idea

Try: "Write me 3 questions for the doctor"

Before an appointment, ask the helper to turn your worries into clear, specific questions. Bring them on paper.

Why we picked this: a Hyde Park reader said it changed her last doctor's visit for the better.

Meet our helper

This is Cindy. She's an AI.

You can ask her to explain a word, summarize a letter, draft a reply, or just tell you what she thinks about a suspicious email. She remembers nothing about you between conversations.

What Cindy can do

Explain things, draft text, and summarize documents you paste in. Answer in plain English.

What Cindy won't do

Give medical, legal, or financial advice — and she'll tell you to call a real person when that's the right answer.
